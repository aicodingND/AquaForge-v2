## 📊 Rate Calculation Methodology

### The Billing Formula

```
Hourly Rate = (Desired Salary + Expenses + Profit) / Billable Hours
```

### Key Variables

| Variable                     | Your Value       | Notes                            |
| ---------------------------- | ---------------- | -------------------------------- |
| **Target Annual Income**     | $**\_\_\_\_**    | What you want to take home       |
| **Annual Business Expenses** | ~$15,000-25,000  | Software, tools, insurance, etc. |
| **Annual Tax Reserve**       | 25-35% of income | Self-employment taxes            |
| **Billable Hours/Year**      | ~1,200-1,500     | 30 hrs/wk × 40-50 weeks          |
| **Non-Billable Time**        | 30-40%           | Admin, marketing, learning       |

### Billing Multiplier Method

If converting from salary to freelance, use a multiplier:

| Multiplier | Use Case                         | Example                              |
| ---------- | -------------------------------- | ------------------------------------ |
| **2.0x**   | High utilization (~50% billable) | $75k salary → $150k gross → ~$72/hr  |
| **2.5x**   | Standard (~40% billable)         | $75k salary → $187k gross → ~$90/hr  |
| **3.0x**   | Consulting/premium work          | $75k salary → $225k gross → ~$108/hr |

> **Source:** [withmoxie.com](https://withmoxie.com), [bqe.com](https://bqe.com), industry consulting standards

## 📋 Pricing Structures

### Hourly Billing

**Pros:** Flexible, easy to adjust scope  
**Cons:** Client may be cost-conscious, limits earning  
**Best for:** Uncertain scope, ongoing work, maintenance

### Project-Based (Fixed Price)

**Formula:** `Hours × Rate × Risk Buffer (1.15-1.30)`

| Project Size        | Risk Buffer | Example                      |
| ------------------- | ----------- | ---------------------------- |
| Small (<40 hrs)     | 1.15 (15%)  | 40h × $150 × 1.15 = $6,900   |
| Medium (40-160 hrs) | 1.20 (20%)  | 100h × $150 × 1.20 = $18,000 |
| Large (160+ hrs)    | 1.30 (30%)  | 300h × $150 × 1.30 = $58,500 |

**Best for:** Clear scope, defined deliverables

### Retainer

**Formula:** `Hours × Rate × Discount (0.85-0.90)`

| Retainer Size | Discount | Example                      |
| ------------- | -------- | ---------------------------- |
| 10 hrs/month  | 10%      | 10 × $150 × 0.90 = $1,350/mo |
| 20 hrs/month  | 12%      | 20 × $150 × 0.88 = $2,640/mo |
| 40+ hrs/month | 15%      | 40 × $150 × 0.85 = $5,100/mo |

**Best for:** Ongoing relationships, predictable income

### Value-Based Pricing

**Formula:** `Client Value × 10-20%`

For projects where ROI is clear (e.g., automation saves $100k/year):

- Charge 10-20% of value = $10k-20k, regardless of hours

**Best for:** High-impact, measurable outcomes

## 🔄 Flexible Engagement Models (For Sporadic Work)

These models are designed for **working on your own schedule** without client resentment or payment issues.

### Model 1: Milestone-Based Billing

**How it works:** Break project into discrete deliverables, bill upon completion.

**Example: Championship Module at $165/hr blended rate**

| Milestone | Deliverable                                                | Hours   | Price       | Timeline     |
| --------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- | ------- | ----------- | ------------ |
| M1        | Psych Sheet Parser (data models, CSV/PDF parsing)          | 51      | $8,400      | "When ready" |
| M2        | Point Projection Engine (scoring, swing events)            | 55      | $9,100      | "When ready" |
| M3        | Entry Selection Optimizer (ILP solver, constraints)        | 71      | $11,700     | "When ready" |
| M4        | Relay Configuration Optimizer (Hungarian algo, trade-offs) | 64      | $10,600     | "When ready" |
| M5        | Integration + Testing + Polish                             | 62      | $10,200     | "When ready" |
| **TOTAL** |                                                            | **303** | **$50,000** |              |

**Why it works:**

- ✅ No hourly tracking or justification
- ✅ Work when you have time/energy
- ✅ Client pays for results, not time
- ✅ No resentment—clear deliverable = clear payment
- ✅ You control the pace

**Contract language:**

> "Work will be delivered in milestone phases. Each milestone will be completed and delivered within [X weeks/as available]. Payment due upon milestone acceptance."

### Model 2: Async Retainer (Bucket Hours)

**How it works:** Sell blocks of hours, no expiration, work async.

| Package  | Hours  | Price  | Validity  |
| -------- | ------ | ------ | --------- |
| Starter  | 10 hrs | $1,500 | 6 months  |
| Standard | 25 hrs | $3,500 | 12 months |
| Premium  | 50 hrs | $6,500 | 18 months |

**Why it works:**

- ✅ Client pre-pays (cash flow certainty)
- ✅ No monthly commitment pressure on you
- ✅ Work at your pace, hours deducted as used
- ✅ Client feels they have "access" without daily demands

**Contract language:**

> "Retainer hours do not expire for [X months]. Work is performed asynchronously with [48-72 hour] response time. Rush requests may use hours at 1.5x rate."

### Model 3: "Seasons" or Sprints

**How it works:** Commit to focused work periods, gaps are expected.

| Season           | Duration | Focus               | Price   |
| ---------------- | -------- | ------------------- | ------- |
| Spring Sprint    | Jan-Feb  | Championship module | $15,000 |
| Summer Sprint    | Jun-Jul  | Enterprise features | $20,000 |
| Fall Maintenance | Sep      | Bug fixes, updates  | $5,000  |

**Why it works:**

- ✅ Matches natural work rhythms
- ✅ Client knows gaps are planned, not abandonment
- ✅ Intense focus when working, guilt-free breaks
- ✅ Clear deliverables per season

**Contract language:**

> "Development occurs in seasonal sprints. Each sprint has defined scope and deliverables. Gaps between sprints are normal and used for planning and preparation."

### Model 4: Advisory + Execution Hybrid

**How it works:** Low-touch advisory access + execution bursts.

| Component       | Description                         | Monthly Cost       |
| --------------- | ----------------------------------- | ------------------ |
| Advisory Access | Email/Slack for questions, guidance | $500/mo            |
| Execution Burst | 20 hours of development             | $3,000 (as needed) |

**Why it works:**

- ✅ Small recurring revenue without heavy work
- ✅ Client feels supported even in gaps
- ✅ Execute when you have bandwidth
- ✅ Clear separation of "thinking" vs "doing"

### Model 5: Completion Guarantee (No Timeline)

**How it works:** Fixed price for outcome, YOU control timeline.

**Example:**

> "I will build the Championship Module for $25,000. I estimate 2-3 months, but the timeline is flexible based on my availability. You pay 50% upfront, 50% on delivery."

**Why it works:**

- ✅ Client focused on outcome, not clock
- ✅ No daily check-ins or status pressure
- ✅ Upfront payment covers your time investment
- ✅ Delivery when ready, not when demanded

## 🛡️ Protecting Yourself from Common Issues

### Payment Timing

| Strategy               | How                                 |
| ---------------------- | ----------------------------------- |
| **Upfront deposits**   | Require 30-50% before starting      |
| **Milestone payments** | Payment triggers work on next phase |
| **Kill fee**           | If client cancels, keep deposit     |
| **Net 7, not Net 30**  | Shorter payment terms               |
| **Late fees**          | 1.5%/month on overdue               |

### Scope Protection

| Issue                 | Prevention                                        |
| --------------------- | ------------------------------------------------- |
| Endless revisions     | "2 revision rounds included"                      |
| Scope creep           | "Changes beyond scope billed separately"          |
| "Just one more thing" | "Additional requests subject to change order"     |
| Unclear requirements  | "Scope defined in SOW, signed before work begins" |

### Time/Effort Resentment

| Cause                | Solution                                       |
| -------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- |
| Underpriced work     | Use rate card, add buffer                      |
| Over-communication   | "Updates provided weekly, not daily"           |
| Unclear expectations | Written SOW with explicit boundaries           |
| Client chasing you   | Async communication policy                     |
| Guilt about gaps     | Milestone model—you owe deliverables, not time |

### Contract Clauses to Include

```text
1. TIMELINE: "Work is performed on a flexible schedule.
   Estimated completion: [X weeks/months]. Actual delivery
   may vary based on mutual priorities."

2. COMMUNICATION: "Updates provided [weekly/bi-weekly].
   Responses within [48-72] business hours."

3. PAYMENT: "[X]% due upfront. Remaining balance due upon
   [milestone/completion]. Late payments accrue 1.5%/month."

4. PAUSE CLAUSE: "Either party may pause the project for up
   to [30 days] with written notice. Paused projects resume
   at original terms."

5. TERMINATION: "Either party may terminate with [14 days]
   notice. Client pays for work completed. Deposit non-refundable."
```

## 🤝 Client Negotiation Guidelines

### Never Go Below Your Floor

Calculate your absolute minimum:

```
Floor Rate = (Living Expenses + Min Taxes) / Max Billable Hours
```

### Response to "That's Too Expensive"

1. **Explain value:** "This includes [scope], and I've successfully delivered [similar work]."
2. **Offer alternatives:** "We could reduce scope to X for $Y."
3. **Stand firm (politely):** "I understand budget concerns. My rate reflects market standards and the quality I deliver."

### Red Flags 🚩

- Asking for free work "to prove yourself"
- Vague scope with fixed price demand
- "We'll have more work later" without commitment
- Excessive revision requests not in scope
