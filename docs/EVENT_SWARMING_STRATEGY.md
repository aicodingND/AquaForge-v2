## 🎯 **Event Swarming Strategy**

### What is Event Swarming?

**Definition:** Loading an event with slower swimmers (who may lose) to **save your fastest swimmers** for events where they have a better chance of winning.

### Example Scenario

**Your Team:**

- John (11th) - Fast in 50 Free (23.0) AND 100 Free (51.0)
- Mike (10th) - Fast in 50 Free (23.5) AND 100 Fly (55.0)
- Sam (9th) - Decent in 50 Free (25.0)
- Alex (8th) - Slow in 50 Free (27.0)
- Tom (7th) - Very slow in 50 Free (29.0) - **EXHIBITION**

**Opponent:**

- All fast swimmers in 50 Free (22.5, 23.0, 23.5)

### ❌ **Bad Strategy (No Swarming):**

**Boys 50 Free:**

- Use John, Mike, Sam (your 3 fastest)
- **Result:** Opponent wins 1st-2nd-3rd (6+4+3=13 pts), You get 4th-5th-6th (2+1+0=3 pts)
- **Problem:** John and Mike are now maxed out (can't swim 100 Free or 100 Fly)

**Boys 100 Free:**

- Can't use John (maxed out)
- Use slower swimmers
- **Result:** Opponent dominates

**Boys 100 Fly:**

- Can't use Mike (maxed out)
- Use slower swimmers
- **Result:** Opponent dominates

**Total:** You lose multiple events

### ✅ **Good Strategy (Event Swarming):**

**Boys 50 Free (SWARM THIS EVENT):**

- Use Sam (9th, 25.0), Alex (8th, 27.0), Tom (7th, 29.0 - exhibition)
- **Result:** Opponent wins 1st-2nd-3rd (6+4+3=13 pts), You get 4th-5th-6th (2+1+0=3 pts)
- **You "lose" this event BUT...**
- **John and Mike are SAVED for better events!**

**Boys 100 Free (USE JOHN HERE):**

- Use John (11th, 51.0) + other fast swimmers
- **Result:** John wins 1st (6 pts), team gets 1st-2nd-3rd (6+4+3=13 pts)
- **You WIN this event!**

**Boys 100 Fly (USE MIKE HERE):**

- Use Mike (10th, 55.0) + other fast swimmers
- **Result:** Mike wins 1st (6 pts), team gets 1st-2nd-3rd (6+4+3=13 pts)
- **You WIN this event!**

**Total:** You "lose" 50 Free (3 pts) but WIN 100 Free (13 pts) + 100 Fly (13 pts) = **Net gain!**

## 🎯 **Strategic Principles**

### 1. **Sacrifice Weak Events**

- Identify events where opponent is much stronger
- "Swarm" with slower swimmers
- Accept the loss to save fast swimmers

### 2. **Dominate Strong Events**

- Use your saved fast swimmers here
- Win big with 1st-2nd-3rd sweeps
- Maximize points where you have advantage

### 3. **Use Exhibition Swimmers (7th Grade)**

- They can compete and displace
- They DON'T count toward 2-event limit
- Perfect for swarming without using up varsity slots

### 4. **2-Event Limit is Key**

- Each swimmer can only do 2 events
- Choose wisely: Swarm weak events, dominate strong events
- Save your best swimmers for events you can win

## 📊 **Math Example**

**Scenario:** 12-event meet

**Without Swarming:**

- Use best swimmers in ALL events
- Win 4 events (52 pts)
- Lose 8 events (24 pts)
- **Total: 76 pts**

**With Swarming:**

- Swarm 6 weak events with slow swimmers (18 pts)
- Dominate 6 strong events with saved fast swimmers (78 pts)
- **Total: 96 pts** ✓ **+20 point advantage!**

## 🎯 **When to Swarm**

### Swarm When

- ✅ Opponent is much faster in this event
- ✅ You have a better event to use your fast swimmers
- ✅ You have slower swimmers available to fill spots
- ✅ You can afford to "lose" this event

### Don't Swarm When

- ❌ You can win this event
- ❌ This is a high-value event (relay, diving)
- ❌ You don't have better events to save swimmers for
- ❌ Opponent is weak in this event
